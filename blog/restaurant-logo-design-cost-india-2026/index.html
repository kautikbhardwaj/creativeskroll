How much does restaurant logo design cost in India in 2026?

By Sanchita Bhatia April 2026 8 min read 2,200 words

Your restaurant's logo appears on menus, signage, packaging, Zomato, Swiggy, Instagram, and everything in between. Getting it right is not optional — and knowing what to pay for it means you won't overpay or underpay for something that represents your brand every single day.

Restaurant logo design in India is one of the most price-varied creative services in the market. A Fiverr logo costs ₹500. A premium branding studio charges ₹1,50,000. And the quality difference between the two is enormous. This guide breaks down exactly what you should pay at every stage of your restaurant's growth — with real numbers, real examples, and clear advice on what to look for and what to avoid.

Why restaurant logos are different from regular logos

A restaurant logo has to work harder than almost any other type of logo. It needs to function across a wider range of surfaces and contexts than a typical business logo — and the stakes for getting it wrong are higher, because customers judge food businesses extremely quickly based on visual presentation.

Consider everywhere your restaurant logo appears: menus (printed and digital), the restaurant signboard, food packaging, delivery platform profiles (Zomato, Swiggy, Magicpin), social media, loyalty cards, staff uniforms, and now increasingly QR code menus and digital screens. Each of these surfaces has different size requirements, colour constraints, and visual environments.

A logo designed without understanding these requirements will fail in at least some of them — and in the restaurant industry, that inconsistency directly affects perceived quality. If your Zomato thumbnail looks different from your menu, customers notice, even if they can't articulate why.

What affects restaurant logo design cost

Several factors push the price up or down within each tier. Understanding them helps you have a more informed conversation with any designer you approach.

Cuisine type and complexity

A fine dining restaurant typically needs a more refined, typographic logo with elegant detailing — which takes longer to design well. A fast food or street food brand can use bolder, more graphic marks that are faster to develop. The complexity of the final mark directly affects design time and therefore cost.

Number of concepts

Some designers offer 2 concepts, others offer 5 or more. More concepts mean more design time and higher cost — but also a better chance of finding exactly the right direction for your brand. For a restaurant investing seriously in its identity, 3–5 concepts is a reasonable expectation.

Brand guidelines included

A logo without brand guidelines is a logo without instructions. If a designer only delivers your logo files with no guidance on how to use them, you'll struggle every time you work with a new printer, developer, or marketing team. Proper brand guidelines — even a simple 4-page document covering colours, fonts, and logo usage rules — should be standard. Some designers charge separately for this; factor it into your budget.

Number of applications required

If you need the logo adapted for specific applications — like a circular version for Zomato, a horizontal version for packaging, an icon-only version for a WhatsApp profile picture — each adaptation takes additional time. Discuss these requirements upfront so nothing is assumed.

What your restaurant logo package must include

Before hiring any designer, confirm that your package includes all of the following. If a designer cannot provide these, keep looking.

  • Vector source files — AI, EPS, and SVG formats. Essential for printing at any size without quality loss. Non-negotiable.
  • PNG with transparent background — for use on social media, websites, and any digital application.
  • PDF export — for sharing with printers and vendors who may not have design software.
  • Colour variants — full colour, black, white, and ideally a simplified single-colour version for embossing or stamps.
  • Brand colour codes — HEX (for digital), RGB (for screens), and CMYK (for print). Without these, every printer will guess.
  • Font information — the name of any fonts used in your logo and recommendations for complementary brand fonts.
  • Full ownership — all intellectual property and source files transfer to you on final payment. Get this confirmed in writing.
  • Zomato/Swiggy ready version — a square or circular crop of your logo optimised for delivery platform profiles.

5 mistakes restaurants make when commissioning a logo

  1. Choosing on price alone. A ₹500 template logo will cost more in the long run — in rebranding costs, lost credibility, and the frustration of a logo that doesn't work on half your surfaces.
  2. Not asking for vector files. If you only receive JPG or PNG files, you cannot print your logo on signage, packaging, or any large format without it looking blurry.
  3. Skipping brand guidelines. Without usage rules, every vendor and social media manager will use your logo slightly differently. Over time this destroys brand consistency.
  4. Designing for the owner instead of the customer. Your personal favourite colour or style should not drive your restaurant logo. Your target customer's expectations should.
  5. Rushing the process. A good logo takes 7–14 days. Logos designed in 24–48 hours are almost always template-based or under-considered.

How to find a restaurant logo designer in India

The best place to find experienced restaurant logo designers in India is through their portfolio work. Look for designers who have designed specifically for food and restaurant brands — not just general designers who claim to do everything. A designer with 3–5 strong restaurant logos in their portfolio will almost always produce better work for your restaurant than a generalist with a larger but more scattered body of work.

Platforms to search: Behance, Instagram (search #restaurantlogodesign #restaurantbrandingIndia), and direct referrals from other restaurant owners in your city.

When evaluating a designer, look at the range of their restaurant work. Do the logos they have designed communicate the right personality for each brand? Do they look original or template-like? Are they presented well across different applications — menus, packaging, social media?

Frequently asked questions

How much does restaurant logo design cost in India in 2026?

Restaurant logo design in India costs between ₹500 and ₹2,00,000+ depending on the designer's experience level. For most restaurants — from cloud kitchens to mid-range dining — an experienced freelance designer at ₹10,000–₹30,000 offers the best balance of quality, value, and deliverables.

What should a restaurant logo design package include?

At minimum: vector source files (AI, EPS, SVG), PNG with transparent background, PDF export, colour variants (full colour, black, white), brand colour codes in HEX/RGB/CMYK, font information, and full ownership of all files. A Zomato/Swiggy-ready square version is also essential for cloud kitchens and delivery-focused restaurants.

How long does restaurant logo design take?

7–14 working days for professional work. This includes research, initial concepts, client feedback, revisions, and final delivery. Rush delivery in 3–5 days is possible with some designers but may compromise depth of thinking.

Can I get a restaurant logo designed online from a Jaipur designer?

Yes — all project work is done digitally. The brief is shared via WhatsApp or email, concepts are presented as PDF files, and final files are delivered digitally. Location is not a barrier. Jaipur-based designers work with restaurant clients across India and internationally.

What is the difference between a logo and a brand identity for a restaurant?

A logo is a single mark — the icon, wordmark, or combination. A brand identity is the full visual system: logo, colour palette, typography, menu design language, packaging guidelines, and social media style. Most restaurants need a logo first, then expand to a full identity system as they grow.
