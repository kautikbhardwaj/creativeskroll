Social media design packages in India in 2026 — complete price guide

By Sanchita BhatiaApril 20267 min read

Social media design is one of the most in-demand creative services for Indian businesses in 2026 — and one of the most confusingly priced. This guide breaks down exactly what social media design packages cost in India, what is included at each level, and how to choose the right package for your business.

Why consistent social media design matters more than ever in 2026

Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook feeds in 2026 are more competitive than they have ever been. Every business — from a local Jaipur restaurant to a Mumbai startup — is now posting regularly. The businesses that stand out are not the ones posting the most. They are the ones whose content looks most consistent, most professional, and most distinctly theirs.

Consistent social media design does two things simultaneously: it stops the scroll in the moment, and it builds brand recognition over time. After seeing your content 10–15 times, your audience starts to recognise it before they read the caption. That recognition is brand equity — and it is built post by post.

The problem with Canva templates: Your competitors are using the same ones. When your posts look like everyone else's, your brand disappears into the feed instead of standing out from it.

What should every social media design package include

  • Custom branded templates — not Canva presets, but designs built around your specific brand identity
  • Correct dimensions for each platform — Instagram feed (1080×1080), story (1080×1920), LinkedIn (1200×627)
  • Multiple post formats — single image, carousel, quote, product, promotional
  • Editable source files — so you or your team can update text and images without a designer each time
  • Story designs — including highlight cover icons for your Instagram profile
  • Regular delivery schedule — designs delivered ahead of posting dates, not at the last minute

Social media design by platform — what each needs

Instagram

Feed posts (1080×1080 or 1080×1350), stories (1080×1920), reels cover (1080×1920), carousel slides, and highlight covers. Instagram is the most design-intensive platform — your grid is essentially a visual portfolio of your brand.

LinkedIn

Post graphics (1200×627 or 1080×1080), document/carousel posts, and profile banner. LinkedIn rewards thought leadership content — infographics and data-driven visuals perform especially well.

Facebook

Feed posts (1200×630), stories (1080×1920), event covers (1920×1005), and ad creatives. Facebook is still the dominant platform for Indian small business advertising.

WhatsApp Business

Profile image, catalogue images, and status graphics. Often overlooked but enormously important for Indian businesses that use WhatsApp as their primary customer communication channel.

One-off vs monthly packages — what is right for you

Monthly packages are right for businesses that post regularly — 3+ times per week. The consistency of working with the same designer builds a stronger visual language over time, and monthly retainers are typically 20–30% cheaper per post than one-off project rates.

One-off projects are right for specific campaigns, product launches, or businesses that post less frequently. Expect to pay ₹500–₹2,000 per custom post design for one-off work.

Frequently asked questions

How much does social media design cost in India in 2026?

Social media design packages in India cost ₹3,000–₹35,000+ per month depending on volume and scope. Most small businesses need the ₹5,000–₹15,000 range for consistent, professional content.

Is it worth hiring a designer for social media?

Yes — for businesses that post regularly, professional social media design typically pays for itself through higher engagement, better brand perception, and more enquiries from social media channels.

What is the difference between a social media designer and a social media manager?

A social media designer creates the visual content — posts, stories, carousels, and ad creatives. A social media manager schedules, posts, and manages the community. Many businesses hire a designer for content and manage posting themselves.
